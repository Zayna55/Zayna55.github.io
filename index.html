Guyana

Guyana is South American country, even though it has a more Carribean culture. Georgetown is the capital city and the largest city. The 2017 population estimate is 773,303, which is a small number of people for a country that is 214,970km squared, compared to New Zealand which has a similar area and an estimated nearly five million people.

Guyana used to be called British Guyana, but became just, Guyana in 1966 when it gained independence from Britain. This tiny South American country is very diverse, it includes ethnic groups such as Indian, African, Indigenous Amerindian, Chinese, Portuguese and other races. There is also a very diverse range of religions.
